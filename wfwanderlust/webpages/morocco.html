Attractions

Hassan II Mosque

+ Boulevard de la Corniche, Casablanca 20000, Morocco

+ Price (adult foreigners): 130 MAD/13.34 USD(mosque), 30 MAD/ 3.08 USD(museum)

+ Completed in 1993, Hassan II Mosque is the largest mosque in Morocco. It was named in honor of King Hassan II and designed by The mosque can hold more than 100,000 people. The museum displays artwork created by Moroccan craftsmen.

+ For more information, please visit Hassan II Mosque

Le Jardin Secret

+ Location: 121 Rue Mouassine, Marrakech 40030, Morocco

+ Price (full): 80 MAD/ 8.20 USD (entrance), 40 MAD/ 4.10 USD (tower)

+ Price (ages 7-24): 60 MAD/ 6.15 USD(entrance), 30 MAD/ 3.08 (tower)

+ Dating back to the 16th century, the buildings on the property have been occupied by Moroccan sultans & close people of the sultans. The current structures were built recently as a result of a restoration project. Come to see the amazing views of the riad (Moroccan courtyard house), the exotic garden & the Islamic garden. You can also go to the tower to get a bird’s eye view of the city.

+ For more information, please visit Le Jardin Secret

Chefchaouen

+ Avg daily budget (budget traveling): 242 MAD/ 25 USD (Budget Traveling Calculator)

+ Location: northern Morocco

+ Blue, picturesque city in Morocco, founded as a fortress during Reconquista, many Jews & Muslims fleeing from Spanish inquisition settled here

+ Take pictures & shop around for souvenirs!

+ For more information, please visit Chefchaouen travel - Traditional crafts, pottery | Moroccan National Tourist Office or Chefchaouen: Morocco's Blue Pearl City
